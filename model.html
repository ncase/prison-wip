You’ve probably heard the statistics a zillion times. The United States has 5% of the world’s population, but 25% of the world’s prisoners. Our entire corrections system costs taxpayers $74,000,000,000 a year. One in 35 American adults, and one in six African-American men, are behind bars, on parole, or on probation.

But these numbers don’t show us the individual stories, or underlying systems. So, using actual statistics on age, education, income, etc... here’s a simulation that connects all those factors, and brings the data to life:

START THE SIMULATION

This simulation makes 100 stories a minute. The triumphant story of a man who gets out of prison, turns a new leaf, and makes an honest living. The tragic story of a young boy who struggles in school, winds up in bad company, and ends up in jail. And so on.

click on a person to focus on their life story →

Mass incarceration is one of our country's messiest messes, but it can be solved! By showing you the statistics and systems and stories, hopefully, you'll get a better picture of the entire problem, and what can be done.
