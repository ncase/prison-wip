Schools & Prisons

[a very early prototype]

The United States has 5% of the world's population, but 25% of the world's prisoners. That's, uh, kind of embarassing.

But it's hard to see the full context of such a huge systemic problem. So, I've taken the national statistics on age, race, education, income, crime, etc... and made a simulation that connects all these factors together:

START THE SIMULATION

Every "person" in this simulation has their own life story, and this simulation gives us 100 stories per minute.

click a person to focus on their story →

By combining systems, stories & statistics, maybe we can get a better idea on how to solve our mass incarceration problem! This simulation will show us that — contrary to what many of us believe — injustice is not the cause of a bad system, it's the result.



Inside The System

The people in this sim, and their lives, are based off the real national statistics for low-income boys & men. (other demographics get caught up in the incarceration system too, of course, but this group is the most at-risk)

So, here are those stats, with sources attached! You can modify the numbers, too — to challenge my assumptions, to use local data instead of national data, to evaluate possible solutions, or even to create your own policies.
