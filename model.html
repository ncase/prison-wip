You’ve probably heard the statistics a bajillion times. The United States has 5% of the world’s population, but 25% of the world’s prisoners. Our entire corrections system costs taxpayers $74,000,000,000 a year. One in 35 American adults, and one in six African-American men, are behind bars, on parole, or on probation.

But these numbers hide things — the individual stories, the underlying systems. So, using actual statistics on age, education, income, etc... here’s a simulation that connects all these factors, and brings the data to life:

START THE SIMULATION

Every “person” in this simulation has their own story. The triumphant story of a man who gets out of prison, turns a new leaf, and makes an honest living. The tragic story of a kid who struggles in school, winds up in bad company, and ends up in jail. And so on.
